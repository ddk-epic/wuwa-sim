# `coordHit` Effect kind: synthetic coord attacks as a first-class Effect

Coordinated attacks are modeled as a distinct Effect kind — `coordHit` — sibling to `emitHit`, `stat`, and `resource`. Same shape as `emitHit` (`damage: DamageEntry`, `icdFrames`, optional `skillType` / `element` overrides), but two extra constraints layered on top: the dispatcher **bypasses matcher recursion** for coordHit-emitted events (they never feed back into trigger matching, regardless of opt-ins), and the output event carries a `coord?: true` display flag read by the log renderer. The two-buff coord pattern from ADR-0019 — a presence-flag BuffDef plus a reaction BuffDef — is preserved: the reaction's effects migrate from `emitHit` to `coordHit`, the presence flag stays a plain BuffDef. Heal-vs-damage routing inside the emit dispatch is fixed in the same change: both `emitHit` and `coordHit` branch on `damage.dmgType` — `"Heal"` → SustainEvent + `computeHealing` + `resolveHealTargets`; otherwise → HitEvent + `computeDamage`. Closes a silent miscompute where `emitHit` with `dmgType: "Heal"` was previously routed through `computeDamage` and surfaced as a `kind: "hit"` event.

## Considered Options

- **One `emitHit` kind, tag coord-ness with a per-effect `coord: true` flag.** Rejected. Tagging every emit on every coord buff is redundant ceremony; the discriminator is the kind, not a decoration. The Effect kind already exists to answer "what kind of synthetic action is this?" — adding a parallel "is it coord?" axis on top duplicates the role.
- **A `BuffDef.kind: "coord"` discriminator on the reaction buff.** Rejected. Coord-ness is a property of the _emit_, not of the whole buff. A coord-reaction buff might also carry a `resource` effect (e.g. "+1 Energy when fires") that is not itself a coord output. Putting the flag on the BuffDef would lump non-emit effects under coord semantics; putting it on the Effect keeps the boundary where it belongs.
- **First-class `CoordAttack` declaration parallel to `BuffDef`.** Rejected. Collapses the presence-flag + reaction pair into one declaration, but introduces a parallel lifecycle, a parallel dispatcher path, and a new top-level concept whose surface area dwarfs the ergonomic win. The two-buff pattern works — what was broken was the engine not understanding coord-ness once authored. Fix that, not the data shape.
- **Sugar/macro: author one declaration, compile to two BuffDefs at load time.** Rejected. Engine still doesn't know about coord, so log labeling and the no-chain rule have to live in two other places. Doesn't fix the actual problem.
- **Single 3-way emit discriminator `emit: "hit" | "heal" | "coord"`.** Rejected. Forces `coord` to choose between "produces a damage event" and "produces a heal event" — but Verina's coord pair _is_ both. Either coord becomes a bundle (the first-class CoordAttack path, rejected separately) or "coord heal" is inexpressible.
- **Two-axis on emitHit: `emit: "hit" | "heal"` × `coord: true | false`.** Closer to the right shape, but the heal axis is already a property of the inner `DamageEntry.dmgType` and doesn't need lifting to Effect-level. The remaining axis is just coord-vs-not — and at that point, a sibling Effect kind reads more cleanly than a tag.
- **Defer the `emitHit`-heal routing fix until a non-coord heal-emit appears.** Rejected. The branch on `dmgType` is the same code for both `emitHit` and `coordHit`; the dispatch primitive is shared. Fixing one and leaving the other broken would entrench a footgun for the next data author who writes `emitHit` with `dmgType: "Heal"` and silently gets a damage-formula heal. The shared primitive fixes both at once.
- **`coordHit` events checked at the matcher boundary** (`matchesTrigger` short-circuits on `event.coord`). Rejected for the dispatcher-bypass approach. The matcher's contract is "match events to triggers" — adding a kind-specific branch inside it bleeds coord-domain knowledge into a layer that should stay agnostic. Enforcing non-chain at the dispatcher's recursion site keeps the matcher kind-agnostic ("the matcher matches whatever it's given; coord events aren't given to it") and concentrates the policy in one place.
- **`coordHit` hardcodes `damage.energy = 0` and `damage.concerto = 0`** (encode "coord attacks don't generate resources" as engine rule). Rejected. Coord-attacks-don't-gen-resources is a real game invariant, but encoding it in the engine forecloses future variants. Authors set `energy: 0, concerto: 0` on the inner DamageEntry — a data convention enforceable by review, not a hardcoded rule.
- **`source: TriggerSource` field on `healLanded` triggers** (mirror hitLanded's default-off semantics for synthetic heals). Rejected. The chain-loop concern that motivates hitLanded's gating doesn't apply to heals; heal-on-heal patterns are not the recursive-amplification footgun. `healLanded` matches all heals, synthetic and authored alike. CoordHit-emitted heals still bypass the matcher (per the dispatcher-bypass rule); `emitHit`-emitted heals flow through normally.

## Consequences

- `Effect = StatEffect | ResourceEffect | EmitHitEffect | CoordHitEffect`. `CoordHitEffect` is structurally identical to `EmitHitEffect`.
- **Engine dispatcher** branches on `damage.dmgType` for both kinds: `"Heal"` → SustainEvent (`kind: "sustain"`, `sub: "heal"`, computed via `computeHealing`, target-resolved via `resolveHealTargets`); otherwise → HitEvent. The branch is shared code; `coordHit` adds two policies on top.
- **CoordHit non-chain rule** lives at the dispatcher's recursion boundary: when an Effect about to be dispatched is `coordHit`, the resulting event is pushed to the output array but not re-entered into the matcher. `instance-store.matchesTrigger` is untouched and stays kind-agnostic.
- `HitEvent` and `SustainEvent` each gain optional `coord?: true`. Only the log renderer reads it (to label the row "Coord"); the matcher never sees coordHit events at all.
- `BuffEngineResult.syntheticHits: HitEvent[]` widens to `syntheticEvents: (HitEvent | SustainEvent)[]`, renamed because the field carries both flavors now. `simulation.ts` pushes the union directly to the log.
- Coord resource generation is honored from the inner DamageEntry exactly like `emitHit`. Data convention: every `coordHit`'s DamageEntry sets `energy: 0, concerto: 0`. Not enforced by the engine.
- **Data migration**: `char.verina.lib.mark-coord-reaction`'s two effects switch from `emitHit` to `coordHit`. No other character/echo/weapon/echo-set buff matches the coord-pattern criteria (`hitLanded` + `actor: "any"` + `source: "self"` + presence-flag condition):
  - Sanhua's three Detonate bursts (`ice-thorn-burst` / `ice-prism-burst` / `ice-glacier-burst`) trigger on Sanhua's _own_ Detonate (`characterId: 1102`, `source: "self"`), not teammate hits. Stay as `emitHit`.
  - `char.verina.s6.joyous-harvest-coord` triggers on `skillCast`, not `hitLanded`. Despite the in-game name, the trigger shape isn't coord. Stays as `emitHit`.
- **`emitHit`-heal routing is fixed by the shared dispatcher branch** — the silent miscompute bug (`computeDamage` instead of `computeHealing` for synthetic heals) is closed, even though no current non-coord data exercises it. Regression test added.
- **`healLanded` triggers** match synthetic heals by default; no `source` field on the trigger type. `coordHit` heals don't surface to `healLanded` at all (dispatcher bypass); `emitHit` heals do.
- **ADR-0019 amendment**: the closing line ("`actor: "any"` on the trigger is what lets teammate hits qualify") still holds. The line in §Consequences that implicitly refers to the reaction as an `emitHit` is now `coordHit`. The presence-flag pattern itself is unchanged.
- New domain term **CoordHit Effect** enters CONTEXT.md under the Buff System. **Synthetic Hit** broadens to cover heal-shaped synthetic events. **Coordinated Attack** is rewritten — no longer "modeled as two buffs with a specific emitHit trigger convention" but "a presence-flag BuffDef paired with a reaction BuffDef whose effects are `coordHit`".
- Log row renderer reads `event.coord` and replaces the source-buff label with `"Coord"`. dmgType column still distinguishes Damage from Heal within a coord pair.
