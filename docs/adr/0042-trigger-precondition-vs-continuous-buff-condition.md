# A trigger carries a fire-time `precondition`; the buff-root `condition` stays the continuous contribution gate

A buff's `condition` is continuously evaluated and gates only whether a live instance _contributes_ — it never stops a trigger from minting or stacking. To express "only accrue while X holds" the data had to set `gateTriggerOnCondition: true`, a boolean that re-points the same `condition` predicate at trigger time. The boolean is a band-aid: it overloads one field with two jobs and hides that the two jobs are genuinely different lifecycle gates. We split them. A **trigger** gains an optional `precondition: Condition`, evaluated once when the trigger fires; if false the trigger mints/stacks/refreshes nothing (and a reaction emits nothing). The **buff-root `condition`** is unchanged: continuously evaluated, gating contribution and `activeBuffs` membership for an already-live instance. `gateTriggerOnCondition` is removed.

The two gates are not collapsible, and the data proves it. `encore.s6` (Lost Lamb) must _not_ accrue stacks outside Cosmos Rave — its gate has to fire at trigger time. `cartethyia.divine-blessing` and `defiers-thorn.amplify` are the opposite: the skill cast always applies the buff, and the `targetHasNegStatus` predicate only gates the amp _while the target stays eroded_ — gating their trigger would drop the buff entirely on a cast against a clean target. Both shapes are `skillCast`/`seconds` buffs with a `Condition`; intent cannot be inferred from event, duration, or stacking, so it must be named. `precondition` names the fire-time intent; `condition` keeps the contribution intent.

Putting the fire-time gate on the trigger is not a second copy of `condition` — it is the home that already exists for this concept. `hitLanded.targetHasStatus` is already a hard-coded fire-time trigger guard ("evaluated once at trigger time, not continuously"); `precondition` is its general form, and the precedent confirms the trigger is the right place. (`targetHasStatus` itself is left in place: it reads the event-stamped statuses during candidate matching rather than live target state through the evaluator, so folding it into `precondition` would shift its source; that consolidation is deferred.) Two fire-time gates that today live elsewhere do converge here: a reaction buff (no `duration`, no instance) whose `condition` is evaluated in the candidate filter, and a `nextOnField` buff whose `condition` is evaluated at trigger time to decide deferral. Both become a `precondition` evaluated at candidate selection, so the engine gains a single fire-time-gate site.

S6 keeps only a `precondition` (Cosmos Rave), no root `condition`: stacks accrue solely during Rave, but each granted stack then lives its full 10s and keeps contributing after Rave ends, matching the skill wording ("each stack increasing ATK by 5% for 10s"). The earlier `gateTriggerOnCondition` fix additionally gated contribution, which silently zeroed surviving stacks the instant Rave dropped — a behaviour the precondition model does not impose.

## Considered Options

- **Keep `gateTriggerOnCondition` (rejected).** One predicate, two meanings, switched by a magic boolean; the field name describes neither job and the trigger-vs-contribution distinction stays implicit.
- **A single `condition` with unified semantics (rejected).** Any rule that stops S6 accruing outside Rave also blocks `divine-blessing` from applying on a clean target, and vice-versa. The two gates are provably irreconcilable under one meaning.
- **One tagged condition, e.g. `condition: { phase: "trigger" | "active", … }` (rejected).** Still one field; a buff that needs _both_ (accrue only during X **and** contribute only while Y) cannot be expressed. The split into two fields is strictly more expressive.
- **Rename the root gate (`while` / `activeWhen` / `presence`) and put `condition` on the trigger (rejected).** A conjunction misfits an object whose siblings are nouns (`trigger`, `target`, `duration`, `effects`), and renaming the root churns ~16 contribution-gate buffs for no semantic gain.
- **`trigger.precondition` + unchanged root `condition` (chosen).** Both names are nouns that fit their objects, they share no confusable stem in use, `condition` keeps its established meaning and its 16 users untouched, and the only new surface is one optional noun on the trigger.

## Consequences

- **Migration moves predicates, not just names.** The six `gateTriggerOnCondition` buffs (`encore.s6`, `camellya.crimson-bud`, `cartethyia.heart-of-virtue`/`mandate-of-divinity`/`power-of-discord`) move their `condition` to `trigger.precondition` and drop the boolean. Reaction buffs and the `nextOnField` deferral gate move their `condition` to `trigger.precondition` too.
- **The `on:` subject must be re-derived per buff, not copied.** A contribution `condition` reads relative to the instance's reader; a `precondition` reads relative to the triggering event's source/target. S6's `buffActive cosmos-rave on:"target"` becomes `on:"source"` (the acting character). A blind copy silently changes meaning.
- **A latent cooldown bug is closed.** Cooldown is stamped before `gateTriggerOnCondition` is checked today, so a gated-out stateful trigger with a cooldown would still burn it. Evaluating `precondition` in the candidate filter — beside the existing reaction-condition and cooldown checks, before stamping — means a gated trigger consumes no cooldown, consistent with reactions. No current buff hits the combo; the fix is pre-emptive.
- **`condition` is forbidden on a reaction.** A no-duration buff has no instance to contribute, so its only gate is `precondition`; a root `condition` there is meaningless and should fail validation. (`hitLanded.targetHasStatus` is untouched and keeps working as a matching-time guard.)
- **A buff may carry both gates.** `trigger.precondition` and root `condition` are independent and may coexist (accrue only when X, contribute only while Y); validation stays permissive of the pair.
