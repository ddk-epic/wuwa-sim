# Stage variants as actionFrame-driven truncations of a single Stage

A Stage may declare optional **Stage Variants** that model a player ending the stage's animation early. Variants are explicit per-stage opt-ins: a variant exists only if the character data declares it under that stage's `variants` map. There is no implicit "every stage can be canceled." A variant is authored as a single override field: its base `actionTime` (the frame the player commits to ending the animation). The truncation contract defined here governs `cancel` and `instantCancel`; `swap` shipped later under [ADR-0018](0018-swap-variant-as-trailing-window.md) with deliberately different semantics â€” it splits cursor-advance from damage-cutoff rather than collapsing them.

At runtime the engine derives the variant's effective duration as

```
advance = max(variant.actionTime + reactionDelay, variantFloor)
```

and that same value is the damage-filter cutoff: a Damage Entry resolves iff `actionFrame â‰¤ advance`, **unless the entry is flagged `independent: true`, in which case it lands at its authored `actionFrame` regardless of the cutoff** â€” used for hits the game spawns as separate entities once the stage commits (e.g. Encore's Frolicking Stage 4 summon, whose cancel-eligible point is the summon spawn and whose hit then resolves on its own schedule). Reaction-delay frames are real play time, not dead padding â€” hits authored within the reaction window land, modeling that even an "instant" cancel can let early hits through. Damage Entries that survive the filter are not scaled â€” energy, concerto, multiplier, toughness, and weakness are pass-through.

**Reaction Delay** and **Variant Floor** are global player-level simulator settings (frames at 60fps; defaults 6 and 15), stored in `wuwa.settings` localStorage, not on the Loadout. The floor models the physical-reality claim that a player cannot execute any swap/cancel input faster than ~15 frames; setting it to 0 disables it. It applies uniformly to all authored variants (`cancel`, `instantCancel`, authored `swap` per ADR-0018). Liberation is not specially carved out â€” Liberation stages do not author variants, so the floor never applies to them in practice; the exception is structural, not encoded.

The Timeline Entry references its variant via an optional `variantKind` field; absence means the full stage runs. The per-entry editable `actionTime` UI is removed; both the Timeline row renderer and the engine resolve action time through one shared helper. The variant taxonomy is a closed enum: `cancel`, `instantCancel`, `swap` (ADR-0018); `fastCancel` is a reserved slot, deferred for the reasons below.

## Considered Options

- **Variants as standalone stages (Stage 5, Stage 5 (Swap), Stage 5 (Cancel) all peers in the picker).** Rejected: it duplicates the picker, breaks the relationship between a stage and its early-exit modes, and complicates `requiresStageId` reachability â€” a downstream stage gated on "Stage 5" should be satisfied by any execution of Stage 5, full or canceled.
- **Variants as a generic engine-side cancel policy (e.g. "drop last 30% by actionFrame").** Rejected: WuWa's cancel windows are skill-specific and not derivable from a universal rule. Sanhua's Stage 5 fast-cancel keeps a different fraction than Encore's heavy attack; only per-stage authored data captures this.
- **Open-string variant labels (`"airCancel"`, `"dodgeCancel"`).** Rejected in favor of a closed enum. The community-canonical variants are stable enough to enumerate; an open vocabulary drifts and defeats UI affordances.
- **Per-variant damage lists (each variant authors which Damage Entries land).** Rejected: duplicates damage data and risks drift from the full-stage list. Filtering by `actionFrame` keeps the full Damage Entry list as the single source of truth.
- **Per-variant scaling factors on energy / concerto / multiplier.** Rejected for the same reason: surviving hits keep their numbers verbatim. The reduction in totals comes purely from dropped hits.
- **Reaction Delay on the Loadout.** Rejected: reaction delay is a player property, not a team property. Exporting a loadout shouldn't bake one user's reaction time into another's sim.
- **Reaction Delay applied only to `cancel` (not `instantCancel`).** Rejected: only full animations queue in WuWa; even an instant cancel requires the player to time the input, so the delay applies to every variant.
- **Keeping the editable per-entry `actionTime` field as a manual override.** Rejected: with stage + variant + reaction delay deriving the value, a manual override creates two competing sources of truth. Action time is now derived, period.
- **Ship `swap` with truncation semantics.** Rejected. Swap in WuWa is **not** a truncation: the stage continues to resolve on the swapped-out (now off-field) character while the timeline cursor advances under the next character. The remaining Damage Entries still land on their original schedule, and the Acting Character of those late hits is the swapped-out character even though On-Field has changed. Modeling this correctly requires the **Trailing Window** concept; truncating instead would silently produce systematically wrong DPS for any rotation that swap-cancels â€” exactly the workflow this feature exists to enable. `swap` shipped with trailing-window semantics under ADR-0018.
- **Per-stage "independent" flag (whole stage exempt from cancel truncation).** Rejected. The in-game cases are per-hit (one summon spawn, one delayed drop), not stage-wide. A per-stage flag would force authors to either split the stage or fall back to truncation when only one of several hits is the independent one. Per-entry keeps the authoring surface narrow and matches the in-game mental model â€” the summon is one specific Damage Entry, not the whole stage.
- **Unify independent hits with Trailing Window (treat them as a degenerate single-entry trailing case).** Rejected. The two share an engine primitive (hit fires at authored `actionFrame` after the cursor moves on), but conceptually they differ: Trailing Window is stage-wide and tied to `swap`'s decoupling of cursor-advance from damage cutoff (the swapped-out character continues the animation in the background). Independent is per-entry and tied to a discrete in-game entity spawned by the stage. Collapsing them would force the swap-trailing drop/padding rules (cancel-capable re-entry drops trailing hits; non-cancel-capable pads) onto independent hits â€” but independent means _truly_ independent: once spawned, the entity hits regardless of any subsequent cast. Two distinct concepts, two distinct rules.
- **Ship `fastCancel` now.** Deferred. Its lossy partial-damage semantics need per-stage fractional data that's currently rare in the metagame and unauthored. Adds later without a schema change.
- **No variant floor â€” advance is exactly `actionTime + reactionDelay` (the original formula).** Superseded by the floor. A stage authored `variants.cancel = { actionTime: 0 }` produced a 6-frame advance â€” faster than any player can physically execute an input â€” and dropped hits the player would actually have landed during those committed frames. The uniform `max` with a configurable `variantFloor` models the minimum input duration.
- **Floor only when `variant.actionTime === 0`.** Rejected. Creates a discontinuity (authored 0 â†’ 15, authored 3 â†’ 9) that has no physical justification â€” the player's minimum input time doesn't care which authored number happened to be below the floor. A uniform `max` is simpler to reason about and to test.
- **Floor cursor-advance only; leave the cancel damage cutoff at `actionTime + reactionDelay`.** Rejected. Decouples two numbers this ADR and ADR-0018 deliberately keep aligned (cancel/instantCancel collapse them; swap separates them with an explicit rationale). Introducing a third semantic â€” "advance for cursor but a smaller value for damage cutoff" â€” gains nothing the uniform floor doesn't already deliver.
- **Per-stage `noVariantFloor` opt-out flag (e.g. for Liberation).** Rejected as unnecessary. Liberation stages don't author variants; the only stages affected by the floor are those that explicitly opted into a variant, and there are no current authoring cases where an authored variant should defeat the floor.
- **Hardcode the floor at 15 frames; not a Setting.** Rejected. `reactionDelay` and `swapFrames` are already player-level settings on the same dimension (frames of input-timing); making the floor configurable matches the precedent and lets users disable it (set to 0) to compare scenarios.
- **Make the floor stack with `reactionDelay` (`actionTime + reactionDelay + variantFloor`).** Rejected. The floor models the _minimum_ player input duration including reaction; reactionDelay is already part of that duration. Stacking would over-count the same physical time.

## Consequences

- The supported `VariantKind` is `"cancel" | "instantCancel" | "swap"` (the last per ADR-0018); `"fastCancel"` joins if the meta makes it relevant.
- Variants only behave correctly on stages whose Damage Entries have proper `actionFrame` values. Most authored data today has `actionFrame: 0` everywhere; the variant toggle is hidden on stages with insufficient data, and a backfill pass is a prerequisite to expanding variant coverage. Note that with the realistic filter (`actionFrame â‰¤ advance`), stages whose damage is authored at `actionFrame: 0` will emit those hits even under `instantCancel` â€” the reaction window catches them. This is the intended model.
- The floor's effects fall out automatically from the existing `advance` semantics. **cancel / instantCancel:** because `advance` is also the damage-filter cutoff, flooring raises the cutoff in lockstep â€” a stage authored `actionTime: 0` with a damage entry at `actionFrame: 10` lands that hit under the default floor (10 â‰¤ 15) where the un-floored formula dropped it (10 > 6). The cancel becomes less lossy, which matches the model: if the player spent 15 frames committed to the input, hits authored within those 15 frames did land. **swap:** `advance` is only the cursor step (no damage filter), so hits within the floor become immediate rather than trailing; total damage is unchanged, and ADR-0018's collision rules key off the floored value.
- The Timeline UI replaces the per-row `actionTime` input with a click-to-rotate variant toggle that cycles through the stage's defined variants. The toggle commits per click; revalidation reuses the existing path.
- `requiresStageId` reachability is satisfied by any variant of the underlying stage, including `instantCancel`. Damage zero is not the same as stage absent â€” `instantCancel` still counts as the cast for cooldown, resource gates, and `skillCast` triggers. That is precisely why the variant exists.
- `skillCast` triggers fire identically regardless of variant â€” the cast happened. Buff Defs that key off the cast event do not learn how the stage ended. If a future buff needs that signal, it joins as a new structured filter rather than reshaping triggers.
- Cooldown rolls on every variant including `instantCancel`. The skill was used.
- The Action Event in the Simulation Log records `variantKind` so the log makes the player's choice visible.
- The Timeline-row / Simulation-Log `+0.Xs` suffix tooltip (ADR-0018) carries a `floor` component that is **mutually exclusive** with `react`: whichever wins the `max` is shown (`actionTime: 0, reactionDelay: 6, variantFloor: 15` â†’ advance 15, tooltip `floor: 0.25s`; `actionTime: 30` â†’ advance 36, tooltip `react: 0.10s`). `pad` from trailing-window collisions remains additive on top, attributed to the entry that pays it.
- TimelineEntry's `actionTime` field is removed from the schema. Saved timelines with stale values silently drop the field on load. Saved entries carrying a `variantKind` whose stage no longer defines that variant silently fall back to the full stage.
- New domain terms â€” Stage Variant, Variant Kind, Reaction Delay, Variant Floor â€” enter `CONTEXT.md` under Core simulation.
- `DamageEntry` gains an optional `independent?: boolean`. Authored true on hits the game spawns as separate entities (summons, delayed drops). The cancel filter becomes `entry.actionFrame â‰¤ advance || entry.independent`; outside `cancel`/`instantCancel` the flag is inert. Acting Character on an independent hit is the original caster; buff state is snapshotted live at fire frame; energy / concerto / `skillCast` / cooldown semantics are unchanged (the hit lands like any other, and `skillCast` already fired at stage start). The hit is **uncancelable** â€” no drop on a subsequent cancel-capable re-entry, no padding on non-cancel-capable re-entry; once spawned, it lands.
