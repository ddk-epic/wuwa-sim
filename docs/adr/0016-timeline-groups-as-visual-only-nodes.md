# Timeline Groups as visual-only nodes alongside entries

The Timeline gains a **Timeline Group** — a labeled, single-row-height header that owns a contiguous run of Timeline Entries for organizational purposes only. The persisted shape changes from `TimelineEntry[]` to `TimelineNode[]`, a discriminated union of `{kind:"entry", ...}` and `{kind:"group", id, label, locked, entries: TimelineEntry[]}`. The engine, `validateTimeline`, `TimelineSummary`, and row numbering all operate on the flattened list (`nodes.flatMap(n => n.kind === "group" ? n.entries : [n])`); groups never reach the simulator. Groups have an open/locked lifecycle: at most one group is open at a time, the open group captures every new sidebar-added entry as a member, and locking seals the boundary against drag-in / drag-out while still permitting internal reorder and per-entry deletion.

## Considered Options

- **Shape B: flat `TimelineEntry[]` with an optional `groupId` field plus a sibling `TimelineGroup[]` array.** Rejected. The invariant the user cares about most — _the entries of a group are exactly these, in this order, and they stay contiguous_ — is free in the nested shape and must be policed in code in the flat shape. Empty groups are natural in the nested shape and awkward in the flat one (no entry to anchor the header render against). The cost of nesting is a one-time migration and an extra `flatMap` at engine seams; the cost of flat is two sources of truth for membership and ordering that drift over time.
- **Bracket markers in the array (`groupStart` / `groupEnd` nodes).** Rejected as a halfway position. With explicit lock-enforcement we _could_ keep brackets coherent through drags, but ungrouped entries between two groups become ambiguous (does the trailing entry belong to the prior group or stand alone?), and the drag invariants reduce to "membership is whatever the markers happen to bracket right now," which is exactly the policed-in-code problem we rejected Shape B to avoid.
- **Restricting a group to a single character's entries.** Rejected. The most common organizational unit in Wuwa rotations is the cross-character burst window — _Verina outro → Jinhsi swap-in → Jinhsi combo_ — and players name those as one phase. A per-character restriction would forbid the most useful label users want to write. The chrome benefits (portrait in header, color-coding) can be derived from `group.entries` opportunistically without the structural constraint.
- **Group boundaries break combo chains for validation** (`requiresStageId` / `comboAllows`). Rejected. Groups are organizational, not semantic. Folding validation into the set of things groups affect would create spooky action at a distance — a timeline that runs identically in the engine would validate differently based on a purely cosmetic regrouping. Users who want phase separation that actually enforces have the engine-real option of inserting a swap or a Movement entry that breaks the chain by the existing rules.
- **Implicit ownership: "this group owns everything until the next group header or end of array."** Rejected. Combined with lock semantics it leaks: a user dragging an entry past a header would silently re-parent it into the next group with no visible boundary change. Explicit `entries: TimelineEntry[]` on the group node keeps ownership readable from the data alone.
- **Recursive groups (groups inside groups).** Rejected. For a damage-sim rotation — typically 10–40 entries — a single level of grouping covers the use cases, and flat keeps the type, render, and drag rules dramatically simpler. Extensible later if a deeper structure is genuinely needed.
- **Auto-lock the open group on save / page reload.** Rejected. Treating "open" as ephemeral session state silently destroys the user's in-progress intent on a tab refresh. Persisting `locked: false` as-is plus a visible at-rest signal (saturated open-lock color, subtle bracketing on locked regions) lets the user pick up exactly where they left off.
- **Multiple open groups (LIFO or most-recently-interacted).** Rejected. Creates the worst-case ambiguity for "where will my next sidebar click land?" — the very question the lock/open distinction was introduced to answer. At-most-one-open keeps the rule trivial.
- **Hover-expanded options menu.** Rejected. The menu contains the destructive Delete action; hover triggers fire by accident when the cursor traverses the row to reach lock or the variant button. Click-to-open matches the convention in every comparable tool (GitHub, Linear, Figma, VS Code) and works on touch and assistive paths.
- **One "Delete group" action that ungroups (keeps entries).** Rejected per user preference: the chosen semantic is "delete group + contents" gated behind the existing `ConfirmModal`. The folder-deletion mental model is preferred over the bracket-removal one.
- **Loud red-highlight feedback on illegal drop targets.** Rejected in favor of silent rejection (no highlight, no-drop cursor) combined with always-visible bracketing on locked regions so the boundary is apparent before the drag even starts. Red-on-every-hover trains the user to fear the UI; pre-drag indication removes most rejections from occurring in the first place. (User noted drag/drop will likely be reworked later.)

## Consequences

- `src/types/timeline.ts` introduces `TimelineNode` (discriminated union) and `TimelineGroup`. `TimelineEntry` keeps its current shape unchanged.
- `useTimeline` stores `TimelineNode[]` at the existing `wuwa.timeline.entries` localStorage key. `migrateEntries` (now `migrateNodes`) wraps any pre-existing `TimelineEntry[]` payload as `[{kind:"entry", ...e}, ...]`. New entries from the sidebar append into the open group's `entries` if one exists, otherwise to the top level.
- Every existing engine and validation seam that takes `TimelineEntry[]` continues to take `TimelineEntry[]` — they receive the flattened view. The flatten is a single shared helper (`flattenNodes(nodes)`) used by the simulator entry point, `validateTimeline`, `TimelineSummary`, and the simulation-log writer.
- Row numbering in `TimelineView` is computed against the flattened entry index, not the node index — group headers occupy a visual row but are not numbered. The display row at flattened-index `i` shows `i + 1`.
- `TimelineView` gains a `kind`-switched render: entry nodes render as today; group nodes render a header row (lock toggle, label, dot menu) followed by the contained entry rows, with locked-region bracketing applied to the contained rows at rest.
- The header's lock toggle is always visible. Open = saturated accent color; locked = muted, blends with row background. The at-rest visual signal carries the "where will my next sidebar click land?" answer without a separate banner.
- A new top-bar "+ Group" action creates an open, unnamed group at the end of the timeline. Creating a new group while one is already open auto-locks the previous one (at-most-one-open invariant). Empty groups are valid and render as a header alone.
- The dot menu opens on click (not hover) and exposes Duplicate, Rename, Delete (in that order). Duplicate is a deep copy: new group id, label `"<label> copy"`, entries cloned with fresh `crypto.randomUUID()` ids, inserted directly after the source. Lock state is inherited, with the exception that a copy of the currently-open group is created locked. Delete opens the existing `ConfirmModal` and destroys the group with its entries.
- Drag semantics: dragging a group node moves its header and `entries` together to a top-level slot only (flat-only). Dragging an entry within its current group is allowed; dragging an entry across a group boundary (in or out) is rejected via no-drop cursor and a swallowed drop event. Removing an entry via its existing ✕ shrinks the group naturally.
- `validateTimeline` is unchanged in logic and signature — it sees the flattened entry list. Combo gates (`requiresStageId`, `comboAllows` per ADR-0015) can cross group boundaries; groups are visual, not semantic.
- `SimulationLog` output is unaffected — the engine never sees groups, so the log carries no group references. Future export formats may choose to surface group structure as a presentation concern, but the log itself remains a flat sequence.
- Saved timelines from before this change load through the migration as a single flat list of entry nodes at the top level, with no groups. Users opt into grouping by clicking "+ Group" on existing rotations.
