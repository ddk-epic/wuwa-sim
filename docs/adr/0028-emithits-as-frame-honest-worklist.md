# One frame-ordered event stream produces the whole Simulation Log; emissions resolve at their landing frame

There is a single frame-ordered event stream, realized as the mechanism-only **`Schedule<T>` module** (`src/lib/schedule.ts`: `Schedule`, `ScheduledWork<T>`, `ArrivalClass`). It owns ordering, the watermark drain, within-frame stability, and the same-character drop/pad/reset collision policy (`resolveArrival`, `cancelResidue`) — but not resolution, which runs in a caller-supplied `resolve` callback, so it imports nothing from the buff engine, damage formulas, or Simulation Log. `simulation.ts` holds a `Schedule<Work>` and the per-kind `resolve`; one queue produces the whole log.

Authored entries keep their cursor-computed start frames (the `react`/`floor`/`pad`/`fall`/`swapBack` math in `processEntry` is unchanged — the entire cursor-advance chain is computed from authored-only state). When an event resolves and **emits** (emitHit, coordHit), the emission is **inserted ahead into the same stream at its landing frame**: `landingFrame = triggerFrame + effect.damage.actionFrame` (`actionFrame ≥ 0` holds by construction — emissions reuse the authored-hit shape, mirroring authored hits' `stageStartFrame + actionFrame`). The walk processes strictly in **nondecreasing frame order** — mandatory, because engine state is monotonic (`tickToFrame` only moves forward, so "the buff state at the landing frame" is unreadable once the clock has passed it). A synthetic therefore computes its stats **at resolve time, at its landing frame**, through the same `resolveHit` path as an authored hit — moving a synthetic later moves its stat snapshot and resource reads after any intervening mutation, so its damage value can legitimately differ from the trigger-frame value. That is the feature: the motivating shipped customer is Sanhua, whose Ice Thorn / Prism / Glacier Burst emits are authored `actionFrame: 14` and land 14 frames after the Detonate hit with landing-frame stats.

**Contract.** `onEvent` / `recordHit` / `recordHeal` return emit-_decisions_ — `{ lifecycleEvents, deferredEmits }` — never resolved synthetic events. `EmitHitDispatcher` is split into `tryEmit` (decision, at trigger frame, owning ICD `icdFrames` and `chainDepthCap` bookkeeping) and `resolve` (compute, at landing frame). Synthetics arrive in the log only via the stream drain. Note: honoring the offset moves the trigger/ICD window to the landing frame — a deliberate consequence of recalc-at-landing, not a bug.

**DFS order is preserved within a frame by deferring only at `depth === 0`.** A top-level emit always defers onto the stream; an in-frame chain emit (`depth ≥ 1`, offset 0) resolves inline inside `resolveDeferredEmit`, so a chain keeps its depth-first order _within_ a frame. `fireSkillCast` drains at its own frame after enqueuing, so a cast's immediate synthetics still log before — and apply resources before — the action event's snapshot. `coordHit` defers too, but `resolveDeferredEmit` skips the chain for a coord emit (`coordHit → coord` is structurally impossible) and coordHit carries no landing offset.

**Emissions are footing-blind fire-and-forget members.** An emission reads **no** Footing state, resolves **no** footing-conditional buff, depends on **no** other emission, and is **never** subject to Trailing Window drop/pad. So same-frame order _among or with_ emissions is genuinely irrelevant — there is no read-after-write an emission can observe at its own frame. This is what makes "emission order is irrelevant" safe rather than lucky, and it is **load-bearing**: the clean separation (no global same-frame mutation/damage rule) collapses the instant a coordHit/creation needs "while airborne" scaling — at which point footing must re-enter the emission snapshot and this ADR must be revisited.

**Trailing/footing reconcile into the stream without a new ordering rule.** Trailing hits and footing commits are also stream members (resolving frame-honestly at their landing/commit frame) and retain their [ADR-0018](0018-swap-variant-as-trailing-window.md) / [ADR-0022](0022-footing-as-team-state-with-trailing-window-snapshot.md) semantics:

- **Drop-on-cancel → mark invalid at pop.** A same-character cancel-capable re-entry marks the character's pending stream items at or past its effective start invalid (validity check at pop; no priority-queue deletion). Sound by causality: a cancel only drops `hitFrame ≥ F`, so by the time the clock reaches a trailing hit's frame, its cancel status is already final. The drop frame is the re-entry's `effectiveStart`, per [ADR-0037](0037-cancel-capable-residue-drop-at-effective-start.md).
- **Pad-on-collision → stays a cursor rule.** Padding decides _when the next action can start_, so it remains an arrival-time `nextStart = max(cursor, latestPendingSameCharFrame)` computation on the authored walk, not a stream item.
- **Footing commit / window-end reset → stream events** at their frames, subject to the same collision policy (ADR-0022).

The **only** same-frame ordering that survives is inside the authored/trailing/footing domain (a footing commit resolves before a colliding authored hit; an inclusive cancel invalidates a same-frame trailing hit before it resolves) — exactly the ADR-0018/0022 behavior, preserved verbatim. **No global "mutations-before-damage" rule exists**, precisely because emissions are footing-blind and independent.

## Invariants

- **Authored entries never reorder relative to each other.** Only emissions interleave _between_ them; pad keeps the cursor monotonic so character A's padded action can never fall behind A's next authored action.
- **Emissions are footing-blind** (above). Guard: assert no emission resolves a footing-conditional buff.

## Considered Options

- **Eager splice — synthetics resolve inside the buff engine at the trigger frame and are pushed into the log immediately after their triggering hit (the original model).** Superseded. The log was emission-ordered (depth-first), not frame-ordered: a synthetic landing at frame 130, emitted by a hit at frame 100, sat in the log _before_ a later authored entry at frame 120. Worse, the dispatcher discarded the emit's `damage.actionFrame` landing offset entirely, so Sanhua's bursts (authored `actionFrame: 14`) resolved _on_ the Detonate frame with Detonate-frame stats — a latent snapshot-timing correctness bug, not just a cosmetic ordering one. Eager compute also meant eager snapshot, which is exactly what landing-frame resolution must not do.
- **Hybrid — only `actionFrame > 0` synthetics defer; immediate ones keep the eager-splice path (shipped as an intermediate, then superseded).** This was the first shipped state and was nearly ratified as the end state. Superseded because two concrete consumers on the roadmap — off-field / coordinated units and the `animationFrames` off-field-damage projection — need one canonical frame-ordered stream, and maintaining two emission paths with divergent timing (plus an `honorEmitOffset` flag whose engine-level and simulation-level defaults differed) was the standing tax the unification removes. The flag is retired; `landingFrame = triggerFrame + actionFrame` is unconditional.
- **Port straight to the frame-honest model in one change.** Rejected as migration strategy. Moving a synthetic later changes its damage legitimately (the feature) — which looks identical to a snapshot/resource-timing bug in a diff. The migration was therefore sequenced through an **emission-order oracle**: first a byte-identical worklist reproducing the old DFS order (plus a log serializer and characterization goldens), then the frame-flip, gated by the law `β-log == stable-frame-sort(α-log)` wherever a reorder crosses no mutation, with the mutation-crossing diffs hand-verified as the feature. The oracle was migration scaffolding, not architecture; the goldens survive as regression tripwires.
- **Host the worklist/drain inside the `BuffEngine` to preserve the `onEvent` contract.** Rejected. Keeping `onEvent` returning computed events means keeping eager compute, i.e. eager snapshot — the exact thing landing-frame resolution must stop doing. It also pulls the authored-entry walk and `computeDamage` down into the engine, entangling orchestrator and engine. The contract change (and the ~46-reader-test migration to a `drainSynthetics` harness) was the honest cost.
- **Same-frame full simultaneity (same-frame authored buffs visible to same-frame synthetics).** Rejected. It lets a synthetic see buffs from an entry that had not conceptually happened when the synthetic's trigger fired — a causality break — and widens the changed surface from "strictly-later only" to "every same-frame collision" for no correctness gain. Trigger-causality tie-break (same frame keeps emission order, realized as the depth-0 defer rule) is retained.
- **Pre-resolve all authored frames, then walk a fully frame-sorted list.** Rejected: pad depends on trailing hits, which are stream members, so a pre-pass that assigns authored frames before emissions exist is circular. The lazy walk avoids the circularity.
- **A global "mutations-before-damage" two-phase rule per frame.** Rejected as unnecessary once emissions are confirmed footing-blind and independent — the only surviving same-frame ordering lives entirely inside the authored/trailing/footing domain and is already deterministic.
- **Dissolve cancel/pad into a mutable priority queue with item deletion.** Rejected in favor of mark-invalid-at-pop — simpler, and the causality proof (a cancel always precedes the cancelled hit's frame) means a validity check suffices.

## Consequences

- `Schedule<T>` (`src/lib/schedule.ts`) is the one worklist: ordering, watermark drain (`advanceTo(F)` pre-drains the queue `≤ F` at every engine-advance point), within-frame stability, and arrival classes (`residue` for swap windows, `ignore` for uncollidable background hits per [ADR-0038](0038-post-actiontime-hits-resolve-in-frame-order-for-all-stages.md)). The former `TrailingWindow` per-character map, the eager-splice sites, and the `ctx.deferred`/`drainDeferred` bolt-on are all gone.
- `engine.onEvent` returns emit-decisions; ~46 reader assertions across the engine/character suites assert through the `drainSynthetics` / `onEventResolved` harness (`buff-engine.test-utils.ts`) that resolves decisions the way the simulation's drain does.
- A log serializer (deterministic text projection — frame, kind, actor, stageId/buffId, damage, active-buff IDs) plus characterization goldens (swap/trailing, coord/emit chain, footing launch→land, multi-character energy-share, same-frame collision) lock the stream's output.
- Resources from a deferred synthetic land at its landing frame, not the trigger frame — frame-honest, and the ICD window follows the landing frame.
- No new domain glossary terms beyond **Schedule**; "worklist" and the oracle vocabulary are implementation history. The domain-visible behavior (frame-honest synthetic interleaving) lives in the **Synthetic Hit** / **Trailing Window** glossary entries.
- **Still deferred:** In-trailing hits remain footing-blind — lifting that (footing-conditional buffs reading the acting character's live footing) is a separate follow-up — and the Trailing-Window-duration override is out of scope. This ADR does not fix hit-scoping (that landed separately as [ADR-0029](0029-hit-scoped-dmg-bonus-via-appliesToHits-hitfilter.md)).
