# First-class emitHits as a frame-honest worklist, sequenced through an emission-order oracle

> **Status:** Implemented on the `experiment/first-class-emithits` branch (not yet merged to `master`). The β behavior — synthetics honoring their `actionFrame` landing offset — **has shipped on the branch, on by default**, as a **hybrid** (see the "Shipped state" amendment below): only `actionFrame > 0` synthetics defer; immediate ones keep the eager-splice path. Sanhua's Detonate bursts are the validated in-port customer. **The endgame past the hybrid is now ratified** — one frame-ordered eventTimeline with emissions as footing-blind fire-and-forget members; see the "Endgame ratified" amendment at the bottom.

Synthetic hits (`emitHit` / `coordHit`) today resolve eagerly inside the buff engine and are spliced into the Simulation Log positionally — `simulation.ts:170/:293/:350` push each `onEvent().syntheticEvents[*]` into the log _immediately after its triggering hit_, before the walk moves to the next authored entry. The log is therefore **emission-ordered (depth-first)**, not frame-ordered: a synthetic that lands at frame 130, emitted by a hit at frame 100, sits in the log _before_ a later authored entry at frame 120. This branch replaces the eager-splice path with a single **worklist** that produces the whole log, and — as its actual feature — makes a synthetic's landing frame govern where it resolves, so it interleaves frame-honestly with later authored entries (the off-field / `animationFrames` projection foreshadowed in `CONTEXT.md` is the first customer). The decisive constraint is §5/§6 of the charter: moving a synthetic _later_ in resolution moves its stat snapshot and resource reads after any intervening mutation, so the synthetic's **damage value itself can change** — which is simultaneously the feature and the one class of bug that is invisible in isolation.

To make that bug visible, the branch is **sequenced through an emission-order oracle rather than ported straight to the frame-honest model**:

1. **α-oracle.** Reify the current recursive dispatch into an explicit worklist drained in **emission (DFS) order** — synthetic glued right after its trigger. This is byte-identical to `master` by construction and is _not_ the deliverable; it is the reference output. It unifies the plumbing (deletes the three `syntheticEvents` splice sites; routes synthetic compute through the same `engine.resolveHit`-at-landing-frame path authored hits already use) without changing one frame of ordering.
2. **β frame-flip.** Switch the drain discipline so a synthetic whose landing frame is **strictly greater** than a subsequent authored entry's frame resolves _after_ that entry. The correctness gate is then checkable rather than vibes: where a reorder crosses **no** buff/resource mutation, the law is exact and automatable — `β-log == stable-frame-sort(α-log)`, every field byte-identical, only positions moved. Where a reorder crosses a mutation, damage legitimately changes; the stable-sort law breaks _there and only there_, those cases are hand-verified as the feature, and they become new golden truth.

**Tie-break: trigger-causality (same-frame keeps emission order).** Only _strictly-later_-frame synthetics are delayed. Everything that lands on the same frame F resolves in today's emission order, untouched. β changes the strictly-greater case alone. This keeps the same-frame ordering characterized in the α-oracle goldens valid byte-for-byte and confines β's blast radius to genuine cross-frame delays.

**`TrailingWindow` stays a producer** for the entire branch. It owns the `fireBeforeEntry → immediate → drainAll` sequence, which _is_ part of the emission order the α-oracle preserves; it enqueues onto the worklist rather than dissolving into it. Whether it eventually dissolves is a later, separately-justified question.

**Compute moves into the `simulation.ts` drain.** `EmitHitDispatcher` / `onEvent` narrow from "compute + return event" to "decide whether a hit is emitted, at what frame, with ICD and chain caps applied"; the drain step snapshots and computes for _both_ authored and synthetic hits by calling `engine.resolveHit` at the item's landing frame. `onEvent` consequently stops returning computed `HitEvent`/`SustainEvent` objects and returns emit-_decisions_ — a contract change the existing suite reads against (~40 assertions over `result.syntheticEvents[*]`), migrated as a budgeted first-class step.

## Considered Options

- **α only — pure refactor, frame-interleaving deferred to a later branch.** Rejected as the _destination_ (kept as the internal first step). Reproducing the log exactly delivers none of the interleaving the experiment exists for; shipping it as the endpoint would be motion without progress. It survives as the oracle precisely because reproducing-then-comparing is what makes β's diffs trustworthy.
- **β directly — flip to a frame-keyed priority queue and move all emission onto it in one change.** Rejected. With no emission-order reference, an intended reorder and a snapshot/resource-timing bug produce identical-looking single-event diffs (§5/§6). The oracle is the only thing that separates "feature moved this hit" from "bug changed this number."
- **Tie-break (i) — frame F fully simultaneous, same-frame authored buffs visible to same-frame synthetics.** Rejected. It lets a synthetic see buffs from an entry that had not conceptually happened when the synthetic's trigger fired — a causality break — and widens the changed surface from "strictly-later only" to "every same-frame collision," which is far harder to validate for no correctness gain.
- **Host the worklist/drain inside the `BuffEngine` to preserve the `onEvent` contract and avoid the ~40-test migration.** Rejected. Keeping `onEvent` returning computed events means keeping eager compute, i.e. eager snapshot — the exact thing β must stop doing. It also pulls the authored-entry walk and `computeDamage` down into the engine, entangling orchestrator and engine. The contract change is the honest cost of frame-honest snapshotting.
- **A true frame-keyed min-heap for the α step.** Rejected for α: a heap keyed on frame pulls a later authored entry ahead of an earlier-emitted, later-landing synthetic, breaking byte-identity on the first crossing timeline. The frame-keyed structure is a β artifact; α's structure is an emission-order (DFS) worklist.
- **`TrailingWindow` dissolves into the worklist on this branch.** Rejected as scope. Its `fireBeforeEntry → immediate → drainAll` sequence is load-bearing for emission order; dissolving it mid-branch means there is no parity-checkable intermediate. It stays a producer; dissolution is β+1.

## Consequences

- **First commit is a characterization harness, before any worklist code:** a pure log serializer (deterministic text projection — frame, kind, actor, stageId/buffId, damage, active-buff IDs) plus ~5 goldens snapshotting **`master`'s** behavior (a swap/trailing case, a coord/emit chain, a footing launch→land, a multi-character energy-share, a plain same-frame collision). These are the tripwire that makes every later step parity-checkable. No log serializer exists today.
- `engine.onEvent` changes return shape from computed `(HitEvent | SustainEvent)[]` to emit-decisions. ~40 assertions across `buff-engine.*.test.ts`, `verina.test.ts`, and `sanhua.test.ts` migrate from "read computed synthetic off `onEvent`" to "drive emit-decision → drain," landing as their own commit right after the α-oracle is green so they assert against known-good drained output.
- `simulation.ts` loses the `syntheticEvents` splice at `:170/:293/:350`; one worklist produces the whole log. `EmitHitDispatcher` keeps ICD (`icdFrames`) and chain (`chainDepthCap`) bookkeeping — §4's feedback-loop guard must survive the move; a re-enqueued emit cannot escape the depth/ICD caps.
- The β gate is two-tier: byte-identical on non-crossing timelines and on every same-frame collision; `β == stable-frame-sort(α)` where a reorder crosses no mutation; hand-verified feature-diffs where it crosses one. The third tier is where §5 (resource/energy timing) and §6 (snapshot timing) are actually exercised.
- Trailing Window (ADR-0018) and Footing commits (ADR-0022) reconcile with — not duplicate — the worklist: trailing hits and `pendingFooting` commits are enqueued by `TrailingWindow`, honoring its drop/pad and commit-frame point-of-no-return rules. Folding footing commits in as first-class deferred worklist events is the last staging step, after synthetic emission is on the queue.
- No new domain glossary terms. "Worklist," "α-oracle," and "frame-flip" are implementation vocabulary and stay out of `CONTEXT.md`; the domain-visible behavior change (frame-honest synthetic interleaving) updates the **Synthetic Hit** / **Trailing Window** entries only once β ships.
- Whole branch is flag-gated; the old splice path is deleted only once parity holds with the flag on by default. This does **not** fix Sanhua's Avalanche (charter Non-goals) — hit-scoping is a separate track.

## Amendment — β's in-port customer is `emitHit.actionFrame`; the resolve-via-`processHit` model

The original draft framed β's frame-interleaving as needing a future "delayed-emit" mechanism with no current consumer. Reading the code corrects that:

1. **The dispatcher discards the offset.** `EmitHitDispatcher` lands every synthetic at `frame: ctx.frame` (the trigger frame) — `emit-hit-dispatcher.ts:159` (heal) / `:196` (damage) — **ignoring** the emit's `damage.actionFrame`. An `emitHit`'s `DamageEntry` already carries `actionFrame` (it reuses the authored-hit shape), so honoring it as a landing offset (`landingFrame = triggerFrame + actionFrame`) _is_ the entire delayed-emit mechanism — one expression, mirroring authored hits' `stageStartFrame + actionFrame`.

2. **There is a shipped customer: Sanhua.** Her Ice Thorn / Prism / Glacier Burst emits (`sanhua.ts:259/:294/:329`) are authored `actionFrame: 14` — intended to land 14 frames after the Detonate hit — but currently resolve _on_ the Detonate frame with Detonate-frame stats. Honoring the offset is a **latent correctness fix** (§6 snapshot timing), not speculative capability. Verina's emits/coords are all `actionFrame: 0` and stay byte-identical.

3. **Unified model — a synthetic resolves through the same `processHit` as an authored hit.** Emit produces a _pending hit_ resolved at `triggerFrame + actionFrame`, computing its stats **at resolve time** (not eagerly at emit). The honor-flag decides the landing frame, which decides immediate vs. deferred:
   - `landingFrame == trigger frame` (`actionFrame: 0`, or honoring off) → resolves **immediately / nested**, preserving today's DFS order — including the live `skillCast`-emits-before-its-own-`action` ordering (`resources.test.ts:303`).
   - `landingFrame > current` (`actionFrame > 0`, honored) → **defers** into the `TrailingWindow`-style schedule and interleaves with intervening authored entries by frame.

4. **Sequencing (refined).** The parity-preserving step relocates synthetic resolution onto the `processHit` path with `landingFrame` forced to the trigger frame: byte-identical (immediate resolve at trigger frame ≡ today's eager compute), but it lands the `onEvent`→emit-_decision_ contract change and the ~40-test migration at the safe moment, before any output moves. **The flip** is then just "stop forcing `landingFrame` to the trigger frame" — at which point Sanhua's three bursts move +14 and re-snapshot there (the hand-verified feature-diff; a Sanhua-detonate golden locks it). Order within the port: `emitHit` resolution first, then `coordHit`, then the flip.

## Amendment — Shipped state (hybrid), and divergence from the design above

The branch implemented the **feature** (frame-honest `actionFrame` interleaving; Sanhua fix) but **not** the full structural unification this ADR's body specifies. The actual end state is a **hybrid**, and a fresh reader should not assume the "one queue / splice deleted / `onEvent` returns decisions" goal was reached. Current-state details and the remaining checklist live in `tmp/first-class-emithits-handoff.md`.

**What shipped** (commits `264f8bc` → `8ba6201`): the log serializer + characterization goldens; a flag-gated authored-entry worklist; `EmitHitDispatcher` split into `tryEmit` (decision, at trigger frame) + `resolve` (compute, at landing frame); `BuffEngine` surfacing `DeferredEmit`s and a `resolveDeferredEmit`; `simulation.ts` collecting and frame-draining deferred synthetics (`drainDeferred`); and the flip — `honorEmitOffset` defaults **on** in `runSimulation`. 910 tests green.

**Where it diverges from the plan above** (deliberate cost/risk trade, not an oversight):

- **The eager-splice path was not deleted.** `actionFrame: 0` synthetics (the majority — Verina, every `coordHit`) still resolve eagerly in the engine and splice inline at `simulation.ts:300/:429/:489`. Only `actionFrame > 0` synthetics defer. So Consequence "loses the `syntheticEvents` splice" and the success criterion "one queue produces the whole log" are **not met**.
- **`onEvent` still returns computed events**, alongside the new `deferredEmits` — the "narrow to emit-decisions" contract change did not happen, and the budgeted **~40-test migration did not occur** (and is unnecessary unless full unification is later pursued).
- **`coordHit` never defers** (`fireCoordHit` always uses the eager `dispatch`). Moot today — all coords are `actionFrame: 0`.
- **Engine vs. simulation default differ:** `BuffEngine.honorEmitOffset` defaults **off** (engine-level tests stay legacy unless they opt in); `runSimulation` sets it **on**. Reconcile when the flag is retired.

**Open decision:** finish the unification (route immediate synthetics through the worklist too, delete the splice sites, migrate the ~40 tests) **or** ratify the hybrid as the end state and amend the success criteria. Recommendation: ratify the hybrid unless a concrete consumer (e.g. the off-field / `animationFrames` projection) forces immediate synthetics onto the queue. See the handoff for the full remaining list (flag retirement, `coordHit` deferral, and a landing-frame snapshot-exactness caveat to validate on a real Sanhua rotation).

## Amendment — Endgame ratified: one frame-ordered eventTimeline; emissions are footing-blind fire-and-forget members

The prior amendment's open decision is now **resolved in favor of finishing the unification**, because two concrete consumers are on the roadmap — **off-field / coordinated units** and the **`animationFrames` off-field-damage projection** — and both need one canonical frame-ordered event stream rather than a second emission path. The β-oracle's parity machinery is **not** carried into the endgame: emission order is held irrelevant, no byte-identical gate is required, and recalc-at-landing-frame is the feature, not a regression to be policed. (The α/β oracle did its job sequencing the _shipped hybrid_; the endgame is past it.)

**Endgame shape — one eventTimeline, walked once, lazily (option B).** There is a single frame-ordered event stream. Authored entries keep their cursor-computed start frames (the `react`/`floor`/`pad`/`fall`/`swapBack` math in `processEntry` is unchanged). When an event resolves and **emits** (emitHit, coordHit, creation tick, heal tick), the emission is **inserted ahead into the same stream at its landing frame** (`triggerFrame + actionFrame`; `actionFrame ≥ 0` holds by construction — emissions reuse the [[Stage]] shape). The walk processes strictly in **nondecreasing frame order** — mandatory, because engine state is monotonic (`tickToFrame` only moves forward, so "the buff state at the landing frame" is unreadable once the clock has passed it). The separate `ctx.deferred` array + `drainDeferred(..., Infinity)` bolt-on **disappears**: it existed only because emissions were not yet members of the stream. End-of-timeline "draining" is just the tail of the one stream.

**Emissions are footing-blind fire-and-forget members.** An emission reads **no** [[Footing]] state, resolves **no** footing-conditional buff, depends on **no** other emission, and is **never** subject to [[Trailing Window]] drop/pad. So same-frame order _among or with_ emissions is genuinely irrelevant — there is no read-after-write an emission can observe at its own frame. This is what makes "emission order is irrelevant" safe rather than lucky, and it is **load-bearing**: see the invariant below.

**Trailing/footing reconcile into the stream without a new ordering rule.** Trailing hits and footing commits also become events on the one stream (resolving frame-honestly at their landing/commit frame), but they retain their ADR-0018/0022 semantics, which now relocate off the dissolving `TrailingWindow` per-character map:

- **Drop-on-cancel → tombstone.** When a cancel-capable entry is processed at frame `F`, mark the same character's pending stream items with `frame ≥ F` invalid (validity check at pop; no priority-queue deletion). The enabling insight: a cancel only drops hits with `hitFrame ≥ F` (`trailing-window.ts:61`), so a cancel can **never** arrive after the hit it would cancel — by the time the clock reaches a trailing hit's frame, its cancel status is already final. Frame-honest resolution and drop-on-cancel do not conflict.
- **Pad-on-collision → stays a cursor rule.** Padding decides _when the next action can start_, so it remains an arrival-time `nextStart = max(cursor, latestPendingSameCharFrame)` computation on the authored walk, not a stream item.
- **Footing commit → a stream event** at its commit frame, subject to the same tombstone-on-cancel. (As built, this went further than "team footing on the stream": a commit firing while its owner is off-field sets that owner's **per-character carried footing**, and a **window-end reset** at `stageStart + actionTime` benches an unreturned In-trailing character to `ground`. Team footing stays the On-field character's value; a swap-back within the window re-enters on its carried footing. This re-introduces per-character footing — the consumer ADR-0022's "snapshot removed" amendment deferred to. See **ADR-0022 → "Per-character footing on the event stream"**.)

The **only** same-frame ordering that survives is inside the authored/trailing/footing domain (a footing commit resolves before a colliding authored hit; an inclusive `≥` cancel tombstones a same-frame trailing hit before it resolves) — and that is exactly today's ADR-0018/0022 behavior, preserved verbatim. **No global "mutations-before-damage" rule is introduced**, precisely because emissions are footing-blind and independent.

### Consequences

- The three eager-splice sites and the `ctx.deferred`/`drainDeferred` path both go; `onEvent` narrows to emit-decisions; the ~40-reader-test migration is back in scope. `EmitHitDispatcher` keeps ICD + chain caps in `tryEmit` (note: honoring the offset moves the trigger/ICD window to the landing frame — a deliberate consequence of recalc-at-landing, not a bug).
- The `TrailingWindow` **module dissolves**; its per-character `Map` and `onEntryArrival`/`scheduleStage`/`drainAll` collapse into (a) tombstone-on-cancel against the stream and (b) the pad cursor-rule. [[FootingTracker]]'s `pendingFooting` carrier folds into the footing-commit stream event.
- **Invariant — authored entries never reorder relative to each other.** Only emissions interleave _between_ them; pad keeps the cursor monotonic so character A's padded action can never fall behind A's next authored action. Lock with a test.
- **Invariant (load-bearing) — emissions are footing-blind.** Guard it: assert no emission resolves a footing-conditional buff. The clean separation (no same-frame mutation/damage rule) collapses the instant a coordHit/creation needs "while airborne" scaling — at which point footing must re-enter the emission snapshot and this amendment must be revisited.
- **Kill criterion — evaluated, result GO.** The decomposition spike (reading `processAuthoredEntry`/`processEntry`) confirms pad/footing do **not** entangle with emissions: the entire cursor-advance chain (`pad` :146, `swapBack`/`computeSwapBack` :149, `fall`/`computeFall` :251, footing `commit`/`applyStageFooting` :144/:167, `stageDuration → nextFrame` :278) is computed from **authored-only** state, and emissions are pure sinks (footing-blind, and they mutate neither footing, off-field clocks, nor trailing state). The sole coupling is the monotonic engine clock — emissions need only resolve in frame order. So the pad/fall/footing **math is lifted verbatim**; the work is plumbing, not a rewrite. Two mechanical items remain: (1) tighten drain granularity — `drainDeferred` runs only at entry tops today (:130), which is the source of the overshoot (old Risk D); funnel the ~4 engine-advance points (`tickToFrame` :255/:205, `resolveHit` :355) through one `advanceTo(F)` that pre-drains the queue `≤ F`; (2) move trailing hits + footing commits into that same frame-ordered queue, with drop-on-cancel realized as a same-character tombstone (sound by the causality proof above) and pad kept as the cursor rule. The `useWorklist` scaffold (`drainWorklist`/`processWorkItem`) already reserves the seam. A correctness regression that can't be reconciled remains the only stopper (no parity gate exists by design).

### Work items (dependency order)

1. Funnel all engine-advances through `advanceTo(ctx, F)` that pre-drains the queue `≤ F`. Closes the overshoot for the existing deferred path on its own — shippable independently.
2. Add trailing-hit + footing-commit kinds to the queue (the `processWorkItem` switch the scaffold predicts).
3. Relocate `onEntryArrival`'s drop → same-character tombstone; keep pad as the cursor rule; delete the `TrailingWindow` per-character `Map`.
4. Narrow `onEvent` to emit-decisions; migrate the ~40 reader tests; delete the three eager-splice sites.
5. Retire `honorEmitOffset` (reconcile the engine-off / simulation-on default).

### Considered Options (endgame)

- **Ratify the hybrid (prior recommendation).** Superseded: the off-field / `animationFrames` consumers are concrete enough to justify one canonical stream now, and maintaining two emission paths with divergent timing is the standing tax this removes.
- **Pre-resolve all authored frames, then walk a fully frame-sorted list (option A).** Rejected: pad depends on trailing hits, which are emissions, so a pre-pass that assigns authored frames before emissions exist is circular. The lazy walk (B) avoids the circularity.
- **A "mutations-before-damage" two-phase rule per frame.** Rejected as unnecessary once emissions are confirmed footing-blind and independent — the only surviving same-frame ordering lives entirely inside the authored/trailing/footing domain and is already deterministic.
- **Dissolve cancel/pad into a mutable priority queue with item deletion.** Rejected in favor of tombstone-at-pop — simpler, and the causality proof (cancel always precedes the cancelled hit's frame) means a validity check suffices.
