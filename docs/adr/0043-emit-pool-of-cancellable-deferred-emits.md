# Spawned per-entity damage is an Emit Pool — a capped FIFO of cancellable Deferred Emits — whose count is mirrored as a resource

Some kits spawn discrete entities that deal damage after a delay and can be converted early — Shorekeeper's Basic Attacks generate **Collapsed Cores** that mature into **Flare Star Butterflies** 6s later, but a cap of 5 displaces the oldest into an immediate butterfly, and Heavy/Illation/Transmutation convert all held cores at once. Faithful modeling is impossible with the existing `emitHit`: an entity's convert-or-survive outcome is only revealed by the FIFO future, so the landing frame **cannot** be decided at spawn, and a pre-scheduled maturation must be **cancellable** when the entity is converted early. We add a general primitive — the **Emit Pool**, a per-character capacity-bounded FIFO of **Deferred Emit**s — rather than a Shorekeeper special, because the same shape recurs across the roster (orb/painting/summon mechanics).

**The primitive.** A character declares one pool: `emitPool: { cap?, maturation, emit }` — `cap` optional (omit = uncapped), `maturation` the frame delay before auto-conversion, `emit` an inline `DamageEntry` payload shared by every member (the same authoring shape as `emitHit`, deliberately — a Deferred Emit _is_ a deferred, cancellable `emitHit`). One unnamed pool per character, keyed by `characterId`; an optional pool key is a backward-compatible add if a character ever needs two simultaneously. Two effects act on it: `spawn` (push N) and `convert` (mature N or `all` now). Spawning is authored per-hit as a new `DamageEntry.spawn: number` field, so the per-stage generation nuance falls out of placement (Stage 2's two hits each carry `spawn: 1` → 2; Stage 3 carries `spawn: 1` on one hit only → 1 despite three hits).

**Conversion has three paths; all cancel the pending maturation.** The maturation timer firing, **cap displacement** (a `spawn` over `cap` pops and converts the oldest — a spawn of N over cap displaces N), or an explicit `convert` effect. Displacement is always a conversion, never a silent drop. A conversion emits the payload as an ordinary Synthetic Hit landing at `convertFrame + emit.actionFrame` — so the convert moment decides _when maturation happens_ and the payload's `actionFrame` encodes _travel time_ (Shorekeeper's 40-frame butterfly travel), a clean separation that applies uniformly across all three paths.

**The maturation timer is a new cancellable arrival class on the Schedule — and only the timer is cancellable.** This deliberately bends the ADR-0028 invariant that scheduled emissions are fire-and-forget. The reconciliation: the _maturation_ (a pool-lifecycle event, not a damage event) is the cancellable scheduled item; the Synthetic Hit it produces on conversion remains a footing-blind, fire-and-forget `ignore`-class member, never dropped or padded. The fire-and-forget invariant holds for emitted _hits_; we add cancellability one level up, on the timer that decides whether a hit is emitted.

**The pool count is mirrored as `ResourceKind "pool"`; the entity store is the authority.** The count is genuinely forte-like, so exposing it as a resource buys display in Action Events, `resourceAtLeast` / `resourceStep` / `resourceCrossed` triggers and conditions, and cap registration via the `forteCap` → `poolCap` pattern — all for free. But a scalar cannot hold FIFO order or per-entity maturation handles, and a resource clamp cannot express "displace-and-convert the oldest." So the ordered entity list lives in a small pool store that is the single source of truth; the resource is a projection the pool's operations keep in sync, and pool ops are its **sole** writer. Consequently `DamageEntry.spawn` does **not** route through `accrueForHit` like `forte` — spawning creates timed entities and can trigger an immediate cap conversion, so it invokes the pool's spawn op (which then writes the count); it is not FR-scaled.

## Invariants

- **One Deferred Emit converts exactly once.** Every spawn yields exactly one conversion (timer, displacement, or explicit convert), so butterfly count = core-generating hits — independent of timing. The end-of-run `drainSchedule(Infinity)` resolves any still-pending maturation.
- **Resource count == entity-list length, always**, because pool ops are the count's only writer.
- **Only the maturation timer is cancellable.** The emitted Synthetic Hit stays fire-and-forget (ADR-0028 holds for hits).

## Considered Options

- **Decide the landing frame at spawn (no cancellation).** Rejected. At spawn you cannot know whether an entity will time out (`spawn+400`) or be displaced early (`+40`) — only the FIFO future reveals it. A spawn-time branch on "am I at cap now?" gets the _count_ right but mis-attributes the passive butterflies to the earliest hits instead of the surviving ones, shifting concerto arrival by the build-up window (~2–3s). For a concerto-critical mechanic that drift is the thing we are trying to avoid.
- **Approximate with a minimal primitive (a `stackAtLeast` condition or a fourth resource), no entity store.** Rejected for the same reason — without per-entity deferral the cap and mass-conversion accelerations cannot both be modeled without double-counting, since a scheduled emit cannot be cancelled.
- **Model the count purely in the entity store, teach the log and conditions to read it directly.** Rejected in favor of the resource mirror: re-uses the entire resource plumbing (display, conditions, triggers, caps) instead of threading a parallel count through the renderer and condition evaluator.
- **Make the emitted hit itself cancellable (extend `ignore` to droppable).** Rejected — it would dissolve the load-bearing fire-and-forget invariant for all synthetics. Cancellability belongs on the lifecycle timer, not the damage event.
- **Reference the butterfly damage from its hidden stage instead of inlining `emit`.** Rejected — `emitHit` inlines its `DamageEntry`; the pool follows suit. The dead hidden stage is deleted once its damage object moves into `emit`.

## Consequences

- New `ResourceKind "pool"`; a pool store collaborator on the buff engine; a cancellable maturation arrival class on `Schedule`; a `DamageEntry.spawn` field; `spawn` / `convert` effects. Relates to and amends [ADR-0028](0028-emithits-as-frame-honest-worklist.md).
- The UI `PoolValue` / `PoolResource` (an energy/concerto bar renderer) is renamed `ResourceValue` / `ResourceValueKind` to free the "pool" name for the primitive.
- Glossary terms **Emit Pool** and **Deferred Emit** added to `CONTEXT.md`.
- **Scoped out (planned second pass):** wiring `convert` to Illation/Transmutation at 5 Empirical Data (consuming `forte`), and the non-Basic-Attack core generators (Mid-air, Heavy/Unbound, Dodge Counter). This pass ships the primitive plus Shorekeeper's Basic-Attack spawning (Stages 1–4) and deletes the dead butterfly stage.
